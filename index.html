Case studies

Results.

Three engagements. Numbers reflect client-reported outcomes.
01 · Case study

$42M of pipeline in 3 quarters.

$3.5M MRR added· 117 opps in one month· Out-produced a 17-person SDR team
Situation

Route was struggling to build outbound pipeline into e-commerce merchants. Their 17-person SDR team wasn’t producing enough opportunities.

What we did

We monitored Route’s TAM for companies whose customers were publicly complaining about lost, damaged, and stolen packages. We analyzed each target account’s shipping policy for weaknesses. We built a bot that visited each account’s website, added a product to the cart, and took a checkout screenshot. All of those data points fed into a set of winning plays.

Result

117 opportunities created in a single month — more than Route’s 17-person SDR team produced in the same window. $3.5M in MRR ($42M in ARR) of pipeline generated over three quarters.

Key partner

McLeod Abbott, Chief of Staff, Revenue.

Engagement

How we run engagements.

A 90-day pilot. Three phases.

01
Build Days 1–30

We study your closed-won, closed-lost, and ICP. We build a ranked target layer — typically 500 accounts with the buying committee mapped by name. We stand up your sending infrastructure: domains warmed, DNS configured, deliverability stack live. Handoff protocol defined so positive replies hit your calendar fast.

02
Launch and learn Days 31–60

Campaigns go live at low volume to protect deliverability, then scale as domains prove out. First qualified meetings typically land in weeks 3–4. Three signal hypotheses run in parallel so we can compare them on real response data. Weekly reviews.

03
Scale and optimize Days 61–90

Volume at sustained level. Winners scaling. Still testing new plays. End-of-quarter review: what worked, what scales, what we’d propose next.

Meeting quality

What counts as a meeting.

Most outbound agencies report against “total meetings booked.” We don’t think that’s the right metric. A bad meeting isn’t harmless — it eats your calendar and, over twelve months, can burn the account too.

For every engagement, we agree on criteria upfront:

Title
The actual buyer. Not an analyst, not a coordinator.
Company
Fits the ICP we’ve defined together — size, geo, segment.
Real 30 minutes
A booked discovery, not a polite chat.

Anything that clears the criteria lands on your calendar. Anything that doesn’t is held back, and we rework the play.
