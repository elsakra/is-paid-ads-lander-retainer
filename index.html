01 · Case study

$42M of pipeline in 3 quarters.

$3.5M MRR added· 117 opps in one month· Out-produced a 17-person SDR team
Situation

Route was struggling to build outbound pipeline into e-commerce merchants. Their 17-person SDR team wasn’t producing enough opportunities.

What we did

We monitored Route’s TAM for companies whose customers were publicly complaining about lost, damaged, and stolen packages. We analyzed each target account’s shipping policy for weaknesses. We built a bot that visited each account’s website, added a product to the cart, and took a checkout screenshot. All of those data points fed into a set of winning plays.

Result

117 opportunities created in a single month — more than Route’s 17-person SDR team produced in the same window. $3.5M in MRR ($42M in ARR) of pipeline generated over three quarters.

Key partner

McLeod Abbott, Chief of Staff, Revenue.

02 · Case study

Meetings at 6.5% of all US restaurants in one quarter.

Two new verticals from zero· $200B+ grocery decision-makers
Situation

Upside was building pipeline in two new verticals (Restaurants and Grocery) where they had no existing motion.

What we did

We built a bot that scans every local newspaper in the country for new restaurant location openings, then emails the franchise owner or most relevant marketing contact the moment a new location is announced.

Result

In one quarter, booked meetings with leaders representing 6.5% of all US restaurant locations, and with grocery decision-makers whose companies collectively do $200B+ in annual revenue.

Key partner

Nick Worswick, President.

Why it works

Why our campaigns book meetings where others don’t.

Most cold email agencies run the same templated campaigns for all their clients. Their emails look the same as everyone else’s. Buyers auto-filter these out when they see them in their inboxes. And these emails are more likely to get flagged as spam.

We build custom software for every engagement. That’s the reason our campaigns land in primary inboxes when commodity agency work lands in spam. That’s the reason Route’s $42M of pipeline came from a checkout-screenshot bot we built — not from a sequence template every other agency is also running.

Route

For Route, we built a bot that visited every target account’s site, added a product to the cart, and took a checkout screenshot. Those checkout screenshots became the anchor for one of the winning plays.

Upside

For Upside, we built a bot that reads every local newspaper in the country daily, spots new restaurant openings, and emails the franchise owner or marketing lead before any competitor even knows the location exists.

Gainsight

For Gainsight, we ran 70 experimental campaigns in 11 weeks to find the 5 that worked — including one that mapped each prospect’s reporting structure and used it in the outreach. One recipient posted the email on LinkedIn as a “masterclass in B2B cold outreach.”
