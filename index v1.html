The problem

The LinkedIn playbook doesn’t work anymore.

The agencies you see posting case studies on LinkedIn are all running the same motion. Same Clay enrichment. Same Lemlist sequences. Same “I noticed you were hiring” opener. Buyers saw it in 2023. They delete it in 2026.

Your SDRs are sending 200 emails a day. Most land in spam. The ones that don’t get ignored.

You’ve probably worked with an agency before. You know the cycle: two weeks of ramp-up, four weeks of “let’s test a new angle,” then you cancel and write off the spend.

Hiring more SDRs doesn’t solve it. The problem is the mechanism.

Engineering.

Situation

Route was struggling to build outbound pipeline into e-commerce merchants. Their 17-person SDR team wasn’t producing enough opportunities.

What we built

We monitored Route’s TAM for companies whose customers were publicly complaining about lost, damaged, and stolen packages. We analyzed each target account’s shipping policy for weaknesses. We built the bot that visited each account’s site, added a product to the cart, and took a checkout screenshot. All of these data points fed into a set of winning plays.

Result

117 opportunities created in a single month — more than Route’s 17-person SDR team produced in the same window. $3.5M in MRR ($42M in ARR) of pipeline generated over three quarters.

Engagement

How we run engagements.

A 90-day pilot, structured into three phases.

01 Days 1–30

Build

Discovery. We study your closed-won, closed-lost, and ICP. We build a ranked target layer, typically 500 accounts with the buying committee mapped by name. We stand up your sending infrastructure: domains warmed, DNS configured, deliverability stack live. We define the handoff protocol so positive replies route to your calendar fast.

02 Days 31–60

Launch and learn

Campaigns go live at low volume to protect deliverability, then scale as the domains prove out. First qualified meetings typically land in weeks 3–4. We run three signal hypotheses in parallel so we can compare them on real response data. Weekly reviews. We double down on what’s working and cut what isn’t.

03 Days 61–90

Scale and optimize

Volume at its sustained level. Still testing new signal plays alongside the winners from Phase 2. End-of-quarter review: what worked, what scales, what we’d propose next.

Meeting quality

What a good meeting looks like.

Most outbound agencies report against total meetings booked. We don’t think that’s the right metric.

A bad meeting isn’t harmless. It eats your calendar, and over twelve months it can burn the account too.

For every engagement, we agree on airtight criteria upfront:

Title
The actual buyer. Not an analyst, not a coordinator.
Company
Fits the ICP we’ve defined together — size, geo, segment.
Real 30 minutes
A booked discovery, not a polite chat.

Anything that clears the criteria lands on your calendar. Anything that doesn’t is held back, and we rework the play.
